1. The US Department of Housing and Urban Development is suing Facebook for allegedly violating the Fair Housing Act by allowing housing discrimination on their platform. This discrimination comes from ad serving algorithims that are serving a select group of ads based on factors such as the race of the user which allows facebook to basically draw lines of where to house people based on race.

2. How the algorthims are able to do this is through filtering housing ads to not appear fo certain people who might be of a certain race, income, or have a disabillity. Facebook gave the option to large ad companies to exclude viewers of their ads based on non-American-born, non-Christian, interested in Hispanic culture, and ZIP code.

3. Facebook settled with several civil rights and housing advocates for a reported $5 million dollars which seems like not much at all to me for a practice that is inherently and brutally racist and uninclusive.
